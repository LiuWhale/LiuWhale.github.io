Biography

Researcher at the intersection of control, learning, and embodied AI.

Jingyu Liu is a Ph.D. student in Electronic Information at the School of Automation, Southeast University, advised by Prof. Changyin Sun. Since October 2025, he has been a jointly trained doctoral researcher in the School of Vehicle and Mobility at Tsinghua University with Prof. Shengbo Eben Li's team. From March to August 2025, he was a visiting student at the Robotics Department of Mohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial Intelligence, advised by Dr. Anqing Duan.

His research focuses on safe robot learning and trustworthy embodied intelligence. He studies how robots can predict risk, revise actions, and execute policies under uncertainty, using tools such as diffusion world models, Langevin dynamics, robust invariant kernels, sliding manifold action rectification, and runtime safety filtering for vision-language-action systems.

Before joining Southeast University, he received his M.S. and B.S. degrees from the School of Automation and School of Artificial Intelligence at Nanjing University of Posts and Telecommunications. He also worked as a general software development engineer at Huawei Technologies in Nanjing, where he contributed to large-scale intelligent video analysis systems and data-processing modules.

Research Directions

Safety mechanisms for robots that learn and act in the physical world.

01

Safe Robot Learning

Runtime action rectification and safety filtering for continuous control, with emphasis on closed-form correction, mixed equality-inequality constraints, and policy-agnostic safety layers.

02

World-Model Risk Prediction

Diffusion world models and Langevin action search for forecasting short-horizon outcomes, evaluating reward-risk landscapes, and improving online reinforcement learning safety.

03

Trustworthy Embodied AI

Runtime safety assurance for vision-language-action models, so high-level task intent from multimodal systems can be translated into stable, executable robot actions.

04

Adaptive Certification

Safety certificates, robust invariant kernels, and envelope-style diagnostics for uncertain dynamics and non-affine robotic systems.
